It's advisable to change one habit change at a time, even if you want to make changes in multiple areas of your life. So that you can focus all your mental energy on making one simple change.

It takes at least 30 days to break a bad habit, so a great way to get started is to commit to a 30-day habit challenge.

  1. Start by taking a journal about this habit. Give a specific reason why it matters.
  2. Identify any trigger that pops up
  3. Write down an overview of the habit with the step-by-step action plan you'll follow
  4. Track your success with a daily metric
  5. You'll need to make a decision at the end of the month: Should keep it or not?

Write down the date when you'll start this habit change.Having an official “countdown” will help you stay on track.

Eliminating a bad habit is like setting a goal. You won't achieve it without having a specific objective in mind coupled with a target date.

The best long-term results come from making gradual changes in your life.

The best way to make a permanent change is to focus on daily, step-by-step improvements.

It's better to create incremental goals where you slowly move away from the bad habit.
